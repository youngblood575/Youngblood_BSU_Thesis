Here are two examples of the math format:
\begin{equation}
  |T| \sim \exp(-\tilde{R}(k)x),
  \label{eq:odanst}
\end{equation} 
and
\begin{eqnarray}
  &&I_{t}(x,t) = \exp(-Bvt/\ell_{s}-vt/\ell_{a})
  \exp{\left(Bvt/\ell_s
      (1-{1/2}(x/vt)^2)\right)} \times \nonumber \\
  &&\left(2 \pi \ell_s / B vt\right)^{-1/2}.
  \label{idiff2}
\end{eqnarray}

You can also display the math from expression(~\ref{eq:odanst})
within lines of text: $|T| \sim \exp(-\tilde{R}(k)x)$, or separate
without numbers: $$|T| \sim
\exp(-\tilde{R}(k)x).$$
